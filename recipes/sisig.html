Description

Sisig is a popular Filipino dish known for its sizzling presentation and bold, tangy flavors. Traditionally made from parts of pig head and liver, it's now often prepared with pork belly for ease and availability. The meat is marinated in a mixture of vinegar, soy sauce, and calamansi (a citrus fruit native to the Philippines), then grilled and chopped into fine pieces. It's usually served on a hot iron plate, creating a delightful sizzle when brought to the table, and is often garnished with raw onions, chili, and sometimes a raw egg that cooks in the residual heat.

This dish is a perfect balance of savory, tangy, and spicy flavors. The crispy and chewy textures of the meat, combined with the zingy citrus and heat from the chilies, make sisig a favorite among locals and food enthusiasts alike. It is often enjoyed as an appetizer or a main course, typically accompanied with rice.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pork belly
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup calamansi juice (or lime juice)
  • 2 Thai chilies, chopped
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: chopped green onions and mayonnaise

Instructions

  1. Boil the pork belly in water until tender, about 30-40 minutes. Let it cool and chop into small pieces.
  2. In a bowl, mix vinegar, soy sauce, and calamansi juice. Add the chopped pork and marinate for 1 hour.
  3. Drain the pork, reserving the marinade.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add garlic and saut√© until fragrant.
  5. Add the pork and cook until crispy and golden brown.
  6. Pour the reserved marinade into the skillet and cook until the liquid evaporates.
  7. Stir in chopped onions and chilies. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  8. Transfer the mixture to a hot iron plate or a serving dish.
  9. Crack an egg on top and mix it in quickly so it cooks in the residual heat.
  10. Garnish with chopped green onions and a dollop of mayonnaise, if desired.
  11. Serve immediately with rice.
