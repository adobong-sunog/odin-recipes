Tapsilog





Description

Tapsilog is a beloved Filipino breakfast dish, a portmanteau of its main components: "tapa" (cured beef), "sinangag" (garlic fried rice), and "itlog" (fried egg). This hearty meal is often enjoyed to start the day with a burst of savory flavors. The beef is marinated in a sweet and tangy mix of soy sauce, garlic, vinegar, and sugar, then fried to perfection. Paired with aromatic garlic rice and a sunny-side-up egg, tapsilog offers a delightful combination of textures and tastes.

This dish is not just for breakfast; it is enjoyed at any time of the day, often served with a side of atchara (pickled papaya) and a dipping sauce of vinegar with chili and garlic. The rich, savory flavors of the beef, the comforting garlic rice, and the creamy yolk of the fried egg make tapsilog a versatile and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp vinegar
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced (for marinade)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced (for fried rice)
  • 2 cups cooked rice, preferably day-old
  • 4 eggs
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: atchara (pickled papaya) for serving

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine soy sauce, vinegar, sugar, and half of the minced garlic. Add the beef slices and marinate for at least 1 hour or overnight in the refrigerator.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp of vegetable oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Fry the marinated beef slices until caramelized and cooked through, about 3-4 minutes per side. Set aside.
  3. In the same pan, add the remaining 1 tbsp of oil. Saut√© the remaining minced garlic until golden brown.
  4. Add the cooked rice to the pan and stir-fry until heated through and well mixed with the garlic. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Set aside.
  5. In a separate pan, fry the eggs sunny-side up.
  6. To serve, place a portion of garlic fried rice on a plate. Add the fried beef slices on the side.
  7. Top with a sunny-side-up egg.
  8. Serve with atchara and a dipping sauce of vinegar with chili and garlic, if desired.
